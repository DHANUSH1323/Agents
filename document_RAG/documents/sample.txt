American Revolution in New York City

Several engagements occurred in and around New York City during the American Revolutionary War. The Battle of Long Island (August 1776), also called the Battle of Brooklyn, was a major British victory fought largely in what is now Brooklyn. The Battle of Harlem Heights (September 16, 1776) took place in northern Manhattan and was a modest American success that boosted morale after the loss on Long Island. The city and harbor were occupied by British forces for much of the war after the New York campaign.
